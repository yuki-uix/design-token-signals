Tokens don't just store values.

The idea

◈ Tokens carry meaning

border-radius: 4px signals precision.
border-radius: 16px signals approachability.
font-family: Georgia, serif signals editorial tradition.
font-weight: 700 signals authority.

None of these are wrong. But combining conflicting signals in the same interface creates dissonance — users feel it even when they can't name it.

⬡ Conflict is detectable at token level

Signal conflicts show up before a single component is built. A warm --color-bg: #fafaf8 combined with sharp --radius: 0px and a cold --color-accent: #0ea5e9 pulls in three directions simultaneously.

The analysis in this repo traces "something feels off" back to the token choices causing it.
